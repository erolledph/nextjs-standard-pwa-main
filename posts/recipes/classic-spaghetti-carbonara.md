excerpt: A traditional Roman pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and black pepper. No cream needed!
tags: pasta, italian, dinner, classic
image: https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1612874742237-6526221588e3?w=800&q=80
prepTime: 10
cookTime: 15
servings: 4
ingredients: 400g spaghetti, 150g pancetta or guanciale diced, 3 large eggs, 1 cup freshly grated Pecorino Romano, Black pepper
instructions:
  1. Boil a large pot of salted water. Add spaghetti and cook until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water.
  2. While pasta cooks, saut√© pancetta in a large skillet over medium heat until crispy. Remove from heat.
  3. In a bowl, whisk eggs and grated cheese until well combined. Add plenty of black pepper.
  4. Drain pasta and add it to the skillet with pancetta (off heat). Toss to coat in fat.
  5. Quickly pour in the egg mixture while tossing constantly to create a creamy sauce. Add pasta water if needed to loosen.
  6. Serve immediately with extra cheese and pepper.
difficulty: Medium
---

Spaghetti Carbonara is one of the most famous Roman pasta dishes. It's rich, creamy, and comforting, yet uses very simple ingredients. The trick is to use the heat of the pasta to cook the eggs without scrambling them.
