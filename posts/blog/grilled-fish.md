# Grilled Fish - Techniques for Perfect Results Every Time

Grilled fish seems simple but requires precision. Here's how professional chefs achieve perfect, non-stick, beautifully marked fish every time.

## Choosing the Right Fish

### Best Fish for Grilling
- **Salmon**: Firm flesh, forgiving, can handle high heat
- **Tuna**: Very firm, can grill like steak
- **Swordfish**: Dense, won't fall apart
- **Halibut**: Firm, mild flavor
- **Branzino**: Delicate flavor, beautiful presentation
- **Mackerel**: Oily, stands up to bold flavors

### Fish to Avoid
- Delicate white fish (cod, sole)
- Thin fillets that dry out quickly
- Lean fish without fat

## Equipment Setup

### Grill Temperature
- **High heat**: 450-500°F (for searing)
- **Test**: Hand held 4 inches above grates—can hold for 2-3 seconds only

### Grill Preparation
1. Clean grates thoroughly with grill brush
2. Oil grates (oil, not fish): Use high-smoke-point oil (avocado, canola)
3. Heat grill completely before adding fish

## Preventing Sticking

The number one complaint about grilled fish: it sticks. Here's why and how to prevent it:

### The Science
Fish sticks because:
- Moisture on surface creates steam, breaks oil seal
- Insufficient heat for proper searing
- Not enough oil on grates
- Moving fish too early

### Prevention
1. **Pat completely dry**: Remove all surface moisture
2. **Oil the grill, not the fish**: This is key
3. **High heat**: Ensures proper searing
4. **Don't move fish**: Let it sit 3-4 minutes before turning
5. **Use long tongs**: Flip decisively, only once if possible

## Basic Grilled Fish Formula

### Ingredients (1 portion)
- 6 oz fish fillet or steak (1-1.5 inches thick)
- Salt and pepper
- Lemon or lime
- Herb of choice (dill, cilantro, basil)
- High-smoke-point oil (for grate only)

### Timing
- **Thin fillets (0.5 inches)**: 2-3 min per side
- **Medium fillets (1 inch)**: 3-4 min per side
- **Thick steaks (1.5 inches)**: 4-5 min per side
- **Whole fish**: 4-6 min per side depending on size

### Steps

1. **Prepare fish**: Pat dry, season both sides generously with salt and pepper

2. **Prepare grill**: Oil grates, achieve 450-500°F

3. **Place fish**: Lay fish perpendicular to grate direction (creates grill marks)

4. **Don't touch**: Resist the urge to move fish. Let it sit 3-4 minutes.

5. **Test for release**: Gently press with spatula. If it releases easily, it's ready to flip.

6. **Flip once**: Using long tongs, flip decisively and only once.

7. **Final cooking**: Cook 2-3 more minutes until opaque throughout (test with fork)

8. **Rest briefly**: Remove to plate, rest 2 minutes before serving

## Testing for Doneness

**Visual test**: Flesh becomes opaque, lifts easily with fork

**Temperature test**: 
- Salmon: 125°F (medium-rare, still slightly translucent)
- Most fish: 145°F (flakes easily)

**Flesh test**: Insert fork and gently pull—flesh should flake easily

## Whole Fish Grilling

### Branzino or Sea Bass (1.5-2 lbs)

1. **Prepare**: Remove scales (ask fishmonger), gut, pat dry
2. **Season**: Stuff with lemon slices, fresh herbs, garlic
3. **Oil**: Brush skin with olive oil, salt generously
4. **Grill**: High heat, 4-5 minutes per side depending on size
5. **Test**: Flesh near spine should be opaque and flake easily
6. **Serve**: Remove flesh from bones at table

## Advanced Techniques

### Cedar Plank Grilling
1. Soak cedar plank in water for 2 hours
2. Preheat plank on grill for 3-5 minutes
3. Place fish skin-side down on plank
4. Cover with foil tent
5. Cook 12-15 minutes (indirect heat)
6. Results in moist, aromatic fish

### Grill Basket
- Great for delicate fish or small pieces
- Prevents sticking, easier to flip
- Less dramatic grill marks but foolproof

### Indirect Grilling
- For thicker fish or whole fish
- Place fish on cooler side of grill
- Cook slower, prevents overcooking outside

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Fish sticks | Insufficient oil, not hot enough | Oil grates, increase heat, don't move fish |
| Dry fish | Overcooked | Use lower heat, check temp at 145°F |
| Undercooked inside | Not enough time | Use indirect heat for thicker cuts |
| No grill marks | Cold grill | Preheat longer, ensure high heat |
| Falls apart | Wrong fish or too delicate | Use firm fish like salmon or tuna |

## Pro Tips

1. **Grill marks perpendicular**: Rotate 45° after 2 minutes on first side for crosshatch (optional)
2. **Don't cook from cold**: Bring fish to room temperature 10 minutes before grilling
3. **Oil grates just before fish**: Fresh oil prevents sticking better
4. **High heat is your friend**: Hot grill creates better sear and prevents sticking
5. **Serve immediately**: Grilled fish is best right off the grill
6. **Save the skin**: Crispy skin is a delicacy—serve skin-side up

Perfect grilled fish is about precision: proper heat, dry fish, oiled grates, and restraint (don't move it!). Once you nail the technique, you'll grill fish better than restaurants.
